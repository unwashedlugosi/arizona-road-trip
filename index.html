New York → Cookeville, TN

◉ 715 miles ⏱ ~10.5 hours ☼ Depart 5:00 AM ET
Open in Google Maps →

Beat the city traffic with a 5 AM start. You'll pick up I-78 west, then swing south onto I-81 through Pennsylvania and into Virginia's Shenandoah Valley — the prettiest stretch of the day, with the Blue Ridge on your left and rolling farmland on your right. Cross into Tennessee on I-81, then merge onto I-40 west past Knoxville to Cookeville. It's a long first day, but you'll gain an hour crossing into Central Time.

  • Shenandoah Valley (I-81, Virginia)
    No need to stop — just enjoy the views as you drive. The Blue Ridge Mountains line the horizon for over a hundred miles.
  • Lunch Stop — Somewhere in Virginia or East Tennessee
    You'll be ready to eat around noon, roughly 7 hours in. Plenty of options along I-81 near Wytheville, Bristol, or Kingsport. Look for local diners off the exits.
  • Knoxville, TN (pass through)
    Where I-81 meets I-40. You're 80 miles from Cookeville here — almost done.

Cookeville → Oklahoma City

◉ 700 miles ⏱ ~10 hours ☼ Depart 7:00 AM CT
Open in Google Maps →

A straight shot west on I-40 all day. Nashville is only 80 miles in — too early for lunch but a good coffee stop if you want one. You'll pass through Memphis around mid-morning and cross the Mississippi River into Arkansas (look for the bridge views). Little Rock comes and goes, and then you're into Oklahoma. Arrive OKC by evening with time for a proper dinner.

  • Nashville, TN (80 mi in)
    Quick pass-through. If you want coffee, Bongo Java or any exit will do. Too early for lunch.
  • Mississippi River Crossing
    The I-40 bridge between Memphis and West Memphis, AR. A brief moment of grandeur on an otherwise flat stretch.
  • Lunch — Little Rock area
    About 5 hours in. You'll be hungry. Lots of options off I-40 / I-30.
  • Dinner in OKC — Picasso Cafe
    Paseo Arts District, 5 min off I-40. Excellent vegetarian menu — roasted beet salad, veggie enchiladas, portobello dishes. A real restaurant, not highway food.

Day

Saturday, March 14

Oklahoma City → Gallup, NM

◉ 680 miles ⏱ ~10 hours (with stops) ☼ Depart 7:00 AM CT
Open in Google Maps →

The best day. I-40 from Oklahoma City to Gallup follows Route 66 almost exactly, and this stretch has the highest concentration of classic roadside Americana in the country. 2026 is Route 66's centennial year, so many of these spots have been freshened up. Take your time — the driving distance is moderate enough that you can actually stop and enjoy things.

  • Pops Soda Shop — Arcadia, OK
    30 minutes from OKC. A 66-foot neon soda bottle towers over a modernist building stocked with 700+ varieties of soda from around the world. Grab something weird for the road. Right off the highway.
  • Route 66 Museum — Clinton, OK
    The best Route 66 museum on the entire route. Walk-through decades of Mother Road history. 20-30 minutes well spent. Exit 65 off I-40.
  • U-Drop Inn — Shamrock, TX
    Art Deco gas station and diner, one of the most photographed buildings on Route 66. It inspired the Ramone's House of Body Art in Pixar's Cars. Right off the highway, 2 minutes for a photo.
  • Lunch — Amarillo, TX
    About 5 hours in. Plenty of vegetarian-friendly spots. Yellow City Street Food has a solid veggie menu.
  • Cadillac Ranch — Amarillo, TX
    Ten vintage Cadillacs buried nose-first in a cotton field. Free. 5 minutes off I-40 (exit 60). People spray paint them constantly so they always look different. Bring a can if you want.
  • Midpoint of Route 66 — Adrian, TX
    The geographic center of Route 66. The Midpoint Cafe has a sign and a photo op. Quick stop if you feel like it.
  • Tucumcari, NM — Neon Capital of Route 66
    If your timing puts you here around dusk, slow down and drive through town. The Blue Swallow Motel and a dozen other vintage neon signs light up the old highway. Even in daylight it's worth a look.
  • Dinner — Gallup, NM
    Gallup is a Route 66 town with a strong Native American art scene. Jerry's Cafe for New Mexican food (green chile everything, cheese enchiladas). Or Earl's for similar fare.

Sunday, March 15

Gallup → Phoenix

◉ 335 miles ⏱ ~5 hours ☼ Depart 4:00 AM MT
Open in Google Maps →

Early start, short day. You'll drive in the dark for the first couple hours, but as the sun comes up you'll be crossing the Painted Desert — one of the most striking desert landscapes in the country, with bands of red, orange, purple, and white striped across the terrain. Continue on I-40 through Winslow and Flagstaff, then south on I-17 through red rock country to the Valley. Arizona doesn't observe daylight saving time, so it's the same as Mountain Time in March — no clock change. You'll be at PHX by 9 AM.

  • Painted Desert (sunrise)
    If timing works, the sunrise over the Painted Desert from I-40 is extraordinary. No stop needed — it's visible from the highway.
  • Winslow, AZ (pass through)
    If you're ahead of schedule, "Standin' on the Corner" park is a 2-minute photo op — the Eagles' "Take It Easy" tribute. But at this hour it'll be early.
  • Flagstaff → I-17 South
    The highway drops from 7,000 feet through pine forests and red rock terrain down to the desert floor. One of Arizona's best driving stretches.
  • Phoenix Sky Harbor Airport — Arrive by 9:00 AM
    Delta DL 791 lands at 10:49 AM. Liz, Sam, and Max. You'll have time to park and be at the gate.
