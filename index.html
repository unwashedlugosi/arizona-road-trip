A big first day that sets up the rest of the trip. Head south from Lamar to pick up I-99 and the Pennsylvania Turnpike, then west on I-70 all the way to St. Louis. You'll cross the rolling hills of southern Pennsylvania, cut through Ohio's farmland (Columbus is a good lunch stop), then push through Indianapolis and across Illinois to the Mississippi. You gain an hour crossing into Central Time. The Gateway Arch will be waiting on the riverfront.

  • Breezewood, PA (I-76 → I-70)
    ~10:30 AM ET
    The "Town of Motels" — a famous strip of gas stations and fast food where the turnpike dumps you onto I-70. Quick gas stop.
  • Lunch — Columbus, OH
    ~1:30 PM ET
    About 5 hours in. Northstar Cafe in the Short North is all-vegetarian, right off the highway.
  • Indianapolis (pass through)
    ~3:30 PM CT (gained 1 hour)
    About 3 hours from St. Louis. Quick gas stop if needed, then I-70 straight west across Illinois.
  • Arrive St. Louis
    ~7:15 PM CT
    Check in, then walk to the Gateway Arch if you have the energy — it's lit up beautifully at night.
  • Dinner
    ~7:45 PM CT
    Lulu's Local Eatery in the Grove — fully plant-based comfort food (burgers, nachos, tacos). Or the Mud House for lighter fare.

The scenic day. I-44 southwest from St. Louis follows the original Route 66 through the Missouri Ozarks — surprisingly hilly and winding, cutting through forested ridges and small towns. Past Joplin you'll roll through Tulsa, then onto the Turner Turnpike to OKC. From there, I-40 west into the Texas panhandle with the best roadside Americana in the country.

  • Missouri Ozarks (I-44)
    8:00 AM – 10:00 AM CT
    The prettiest stretch of I-44. Forested ridges, small towns, winding highway.
  • Tulsa, OK
    ~12:00 PM CT
    Art Deco skyline from the highway. Good lunch — Chimera Cafe downtown has great vegetarian options.
  • Pops Soda Shop — Arcadia, OK
    ~2:30 PM CT
    A 66-foot neon soda bottle over a building stocked with 700+ varieties of soda. Grab something weird for the road.
  • Route 66 Museum — Clinton, OK
    ~4:00 PM CT
    The best Route 66 museum on the entire route. Walk-through decades of Mother Road history. Open until 5 PM — you'll make it.
  • U-Drop Inn — Shamrock, TX
    ~5:30 PM CT
    Art Deco gas station that inspired Ramone's in Pixar's Cars. 2 minutes for a photo.
  • Arrive Amarillo
    ~6:30 PM CT
    Check in. Yellow City Street Food or Crush Wine Bar for dinner.

The final push — and visually the best. Cadillac Ranch right outside Amarillo, then across New Mexico through Tucumcari's neon-lit main street. The terrain transforms: flat Texas panhandle, red mesas, Painted Desert stripes, ponderosa pines at Flagstaff, then the dramatic I-17 descent into the Valley. Arrive Saturday evening with nothing to do Sunday but sleep in.

  • Cadillac Ranch — Amarillo, TX
    ~7:15 AM CT
    Ten vintage Cadillacs buried nose-first in a cotton field. Free. Exit 60 off I-40. Quick morning stop.
  • Tucumcari, NM — Neon Capital of Route 66
    ~9:00 AM MT (gained 1 hour)
    Cross into Mountain Time. Blue Swallow Motel and vintage neon signs line the old highway.
  • Lunch — Albuquerque, NM
    ~11:30 AM MT
    Old Town — Seasons Rotisserie & Grill for vegetarian green chile dishes.
  • Painted Desert
    ~2:30 PM AZ
    Bands of red, orange, purple, and white. Even better in afternoon light than at dawn.
  • Flagstaff → I-17 South
    ~3:45 PM AZ
    Drops from 7,000 feet through pine forests and red rock terrain to the desert floor.
  • Arrive Tempe, AZ
    ~5:30 PM AZ
    Done! Check in near the airport. Green New American Vegetarian on Mill Ave for dinner.

The longest day of the trip, but it pays off big on Friday. Same start as Option A — south to the turnpike, I-70 west through Columbus and Indianapolis — but instead of stopping in St. Louis, you blow right through (wave at the Arch from the highway) and continue on I-44 into the Missouri Ozarks. Springfield is the gateway to Route 66 country, deep in the Ozark hills. You gain an hour crossing into Central Time.

  • Breezewood, PA (I-76 → I-70)
    ~10:30 AM ET
    The "Town of Motels" — quick gas stop where the turnpike meets I-70.
  • Lunch — Columbus, OH
    ~1:30 PM ET
    Northstar Cafe in the Short North — all-vegetarian, right off the highway.
  • Indianapolis (pass through)
    ~3:30 PM CT (gained 1 hour)
    Quick gas stop, then I-70 west across Illinois.
  • St. Louis — Gateway Arch (drive-by)
    ~6:15 PM CT
    The Arch is clearly visible from I-70 as you cross the Mississippi. You're pushing through tonight, but the view from the highway is still impressive.
  • Missouri Ozarks (I-44)
    6:30 PM – 8:30 PM CT
    The last stretch. I-44 winds through forested ridges and small towns. You'll be driving into the sunset.
  • Arrive Springfield, MO
    ~8:45 PM CT
    Done with the hard part. Check in, grab a late dinner, crash. Tomorrow is easy.
  • Late Dinner
    ~9:15 PM CT
    The Order (plant-based comfort food) or Thai Express for something quick. Most places open until 10.

The payoff for yesterday's push — a short day with plenty of time to actually stop and enjoy things. I-44 takes you through Joplin and into Oklahoma, where you pass through Tulsa and pick up the Turner Turnpike to OKC. From there it's I-40 west through Route 66's greatest hits. With only 8 hours of driving, you can linger at every stop.

  • Joplin, MO → Oklahoma line
    ~9:30 AM CT
    Cross into Oklahoma. The Ozark hills flatten out and the Great Plains begin.
  • Tulsa, OK
    ~10:30 AM CT
    Art Deco skyline. With time to spare, exit for the Blue Dome district or Chimera Cafe for an early lunch.
  • Pops Soda Shop — Arcadia, OK
    ~12:30 PM CT
    A 66-foot neon soda bottle over a building stocked with 700+ varieties of soda. Take your time — no rush today.
  • Oklahoma City (pass through)
    ~1:00 PM CT
    Quick gas, then I-40 west.
  • Route 66 Museum — Clinton, OK
    ~2:00 PM CT
    The best Route 66 museum on the entire route. Plenty of time — spend 30-45 minutes if you like.
  • U-Drop Inn — Shamrock, TX
    ~3:30 PM CT
    Art Deco gas station that inspired Ramone's in Pixar's Cars. Walk around, take photos.
  • Arrive Amarillo
    ~5:00 PM CT
    Early arrival! Check in, clean up, then a proper dinner out.
  • Dinner — Amarillo
    ~6:00 PM CT
    Yellow City Street Food for vegetarian plates, or Crush Wine Bar for something nicer. You've earned a sit-down meal.
