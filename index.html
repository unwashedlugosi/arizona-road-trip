Lamar, PA → St. Louis

◉ 640 miles ⏱ ~9.5 hours ☼ Depart 8:45 AM ET

A big first day that sets up the rest of the trip nicely. Head south from Lamar to pick up I-99 and the Pennsylvania Turnpike, then west on I-70 all the way to St. Louis. You'll cross the rolling hills of southern Pennsylvania, cut through Ohio's farmland (Columbus is a good lunch stop), then push through Indianapolis and across Illinois to the Mississippi. You'll gain an hour crossing into Central Time. The Gateway Arch will be waiting for you on the riverfront.

  • Breezewood, PA (I-76 → I-70)
    ~10:30 AM ET
    The "Town of Motels" — a famous strip of gas stations and fast food where the turnpike dumps you onto I-70. Quick gas stop.
  • Lunch — Columbus, OH
    ~1:30 PM ET
    About 5 hours in. The Short North district has good food — Northstar Cafe is an all-vegetarian spot right off the highway.
  • Indianapolis (pass through)
    ~3:30 PM CT (gained 1 hour)
    You're about 3 hours from St. Louis here. Quick gas stop if needed, then I-70 straight west across Illinois.
  • Effingham, IL
    ~5:00 PM CT
    Stretch break. You're about 100 miles from St. Louis — almost there.
  • Arrive St. Louis
    ~7:15 PM CT
    Check in, then walk down to the Gateway Arch if you have the energy — it's lit up beautifully at night. Or just grab dinner and rest.
  • Dinner
    ~7:45 PM CT
    Lulu's Local Eatery in the Grove neighborhood — fully plant-based comfort food (burgers, nachos, tacos). Or the Mud House for lighter fare.

St. Louis → Amarillo, TX

◉ 700 miles ⏱ ~10 hours ☼ Depart 7:00 AM CT
Open in Apple Maps →

The scenic day. I-44 southwest from St. Louis follows the original Route 66 through the Missouri Ozarks — surprisingly hilly and winding, cutting through forested ridges and small towns. Past Joplin you'll roll through Tulsa, then onto the Turner Turnpike to OKC. From there, I-40 west into the Texas panhandle. This stretch has more Route 66 roadside stops than anywhere else in the country.

  • Missouri Ozarks (I-44)
    8:00 AM – 10:00 AM CT
    The prettiest stretch of I-44. Forested ridges, small towns, winding highway through the hills. Just enjoy the drive.
  • Joplin, MO → Oklahoma line
    ~10:30 AM CT
    Cross into Oklahoma. The hills flatten out and you can feel the Great Plains starting.
  • Tulsa, OK (pass through)
    ~12:00 PM CT
    Art Deco skyline visible from the highway. Good lunch stop — Chimera Cafe downtown has great vegetarian options if you want to exit briefly.
  • Pops Soda Shop — Arcadia, OK
    ~2:30 PM CT
    30 minutes east of OKC on Route 66. A 66-foot neon soda bottle towers over a modernist building stocked with 700+ varieties of soda. Grab something weird for the road.
  • Oklahoma City (pass through)
    ~3:00 PM CT
    Quick gas stop, then I-40 west into the Texas panhandle.
  • Route 66 Museum — Clinton, OK
    ~4:00 PM CT
    The best Route 66 museum on the entire route. Walk-through decades of Mother Road history. 20-30 minutes. Exit 65 off I-40. Open until 5 PM — you'll make it.
  • U-Drop Inn — Shamrock, TX
    ~5:30 PM CT
    Art Deco gas station and diner, one of the most photographed buildings on Route 66. Inspired Ramone's in Pixar's Cars. 2 minutes for a photo.
  • Arrive Amarillo, TX
    ~6:30 PM CT
    Check in. Good Tex-Mex in town — Crush Wine Bar & Deli has vegetarian plates, or Yellow City Street Food for a more casual vibe.

Saturday, March 14

Amarillo → Tempe, AZ

◉ 660 miles ⏱ ~9.5 hours (with stops) ☼ Depart 7:00 AM CT
Open in Apple Maps →

The final push — and one of the most visually stunning drives in the country. Start with Cadillac Ranch right outside Amarillo, then cross into New Mexico through Tucumcari's neon-lit main street. The terrain transforms as you go: flat Texas panhandle gives way to red mesas, then the high desert of New Mexico, then the Painted Desert's psychedelic stripes, ponderosa pines at Flagstaff, and finally the dramatic I-17 descent through red rock country into the Valley of the Sun. You'll arrive Saturday evening with nothing to do Sunday but sleep in and drive 15 minutes to the airport.

  • Cadillac Ranch — Amarillo, TX
    ~7:15 AM CT
    Ten vintage Cadillacs buried nose-first in a cotton field. Free. 5 minutes off I-40 west (exit 60). People spray paint them constantly. Bring a can if you want. Quick morning stop on the way out.
  • Midpoint of Route 66 — Adrian, TX
    ~7:45 AM CT
    The geographic center of Route 66. The Midpoint Cafe has a sign and a photo op. Quick stop if you feel like it.
  • Tucumcari, NM — Neon Capital of Route 66
    ~9:00 AM MT (gained 1 hour)
    Cross into Mountain Time. The Blue Swallow Motel and a dozen other vintage neon signs line the old highway. Worth a slow drive through town.
  • Lunch — Albuquerque, NM
    ~11:30 AM MT
    About 4.5 hours in. Old Town has great New Mexican food — Seasons Rotisserie & Grill for vegetarian green chile dishes.
  • Gallup, NM (pass through)
    ~1:30 PM MT
    Route 66 town with a strong Native American art scene. Quick stretch if needed.
  • Painted Desert & Petrified Forest
    ~2:30 PM AZ
    Bands of red, orange, purple, and white striped across the terrain. Visible from I-40 but even better in afternoon light than at dawn. No stop needed — it's gorgeous from the car.
  • Winslow, AZ
    ~3:00 PM AZ
    "Standin' on the Corner" park — the Eagles' "Take It Easy" tribute. Quick photo op.
  • Flagstaff → I-17 South
    ~3:45 PM AZ
    The highway drops from 7,000 feet through pine forests and red rock terrain down to the desert floor. One of Arizona's best driving stretches.
  • Arrive Tempe, AZ
    ~5:30 PM AZ
    Done! Check in near the airport, find dinner, relax. You earned it.
  • Dinner — Tempe
    ~6:30 PM AZ
    Green New American Vegetarian on Mill Ave — one of the best vegetarian restaurants in the Phoenix area. Comfort food done right.
